The elements describe the fundamental energy and consciousness of the signs.

Fire (Aries, Leo, Sagittarius): Fire signs are characterized by their passion, energy, and loudness. When reacting to an intense emotional situation, a fire sign's emotional body escalates into drama and anger. Their life lesson involves being seen and expressing their passions without needing external applause.

Earth (Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn): Earth signs are inherently grounded and practical. Under pressure, they become pragmatic, focusing on problem-solving and results while sometimes disregarding the emotional experience. Their life lessons often revolve around money, security, and respecting the physical realm.

Air (Gemini, Libra, Aquarius): Air signs are cerebral, communicative thinkers. When pushed, they retreat into their heads and try to logically explain their feelings. Their primary lessons in life are centered on relationships and communication.

Water (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces): Water signs can't help but be sensitive. In an intense emotional confrontation, they are likely to go quiet and withdraw completely. Their life lesson is to feel their feelings, set boundaries, and maintain faith in love despite heartbreaks.

The Twelve Houses üè†
The houses represent the different arenas of your life where the energies of the planets and signs play out. The house your sun is in provides a central theme for your entire lifetime.

First House (Aries): The house of the self. It governs your appearance, ego, independence, and physical life-force.

Second House (Taurus): The house of possessions and values. It relates to money, investments, security, and what you hold dear.

Third House (Gemini): The house of communication. It covers learning, intellect, siblings, marketing, and short-distance travel.

Fourth House (Cancer): The house of home and family. This area is about ancestry, traditions, roots, and your private, emotional life.

Fifth House (Leo): The house of romance and creativity. It rules children, drama, games, art, and self-expression.

Sixth House (Virgo): The house of health and service. This house deals with daily routines, work, organization, and wellness.

Seventh House (Libra): The house of partnerships. It governs marriage, relationships, harmony, justice, and collaboration.

Eighth House (Scorpio): The house of transformation. It rules birth, death, sex, taxes, inheritance, and the unconscious mind.

Ninth House (Sagittarius): The house of philosophy and travel. It covers the search for truth, higher education, religion, and long journeys.

Tenth House (Capricorn): The house of career and social status. This house is about ambition, success, fame, and your public life.

Eleventh House (Aquarius): The house of community and friendship. It relates to activism, humanitarianism, the future, and technology that connects people.

Twelfth House (Pisces): The house of the unconscious. It rules the mystic, creativity, secrets, and all things that go on beneath the surface.

Low Road: Stubbornness, becoming stuck in routines, believing the negative voices of their inner gremlins, and devaluing themselves.

High Road: Being a rock of stability for others, a generous provider, and learning to let go of attachments with grace. They find peace in simplicity and are devoted to those they love.

High Road: Transforming their own wounds into wisdom and becoming powerful healers who can guide others through darkness. The evolved Scorpio is fearless and uses their intensity for the good of others.

High Road: Turning pain into wisdom and becoming great teachers who can explain difficult truths in a comforting way. They inspire others with their contagious joy and faith in life.

High Road: Becoming a benevolent leader with integrity who uses their ambition for humanitarian goals. The evolved Capricorn learns humility and can admit when they have made a mistake.

High Road: Using their brilliance for the collective good. They are innovators and visionaries who can lead humanity toward a better future by questioning old paradigms.

Life Lesson: To study and connect with the emotional body. Their challenge is to move from thinking about feelings to actually feeling them, which will help them feel more human and connected.
